Introduction

“Taal” or “tala” means to clap. It is the standard musical meter used in South Asian music. An easier way to understand the idea of a taal is to think of how you would tap your feet or bob your head along a song that you really like. What you’re doing there is that you’re, unconsciously, finding the “pulse” of the song and then moving along with it as if the song had induced it’s rhythm into you by resonance. This is the “taal” of that song. It is the rhythmic cycle of a piece of music represented by a person in the form of claps instead of head bobs or tapped feet.
